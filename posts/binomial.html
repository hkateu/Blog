Binomial Distribution

The binomial distribution is used when there are exactly two mutually exclusive outcomes of a trial. These outcomes are appropriately labelled "Success" and "Failure". The binomial distribution is used to obtain the probability of observing x successes in N trials, with the probability if success on a single trial denoted by P.

The examples below will give you an understading of the binomial distribution using the Numpy and Scipy python libraries

Example

What is the probability that 8 out of 15 registered Ugandan parliamentary members will fail to vote on an upcoming parliamentary bill if the probability of any individual's not voting is 0.30 and if people decide independently of each other whether or not to vote?

  • n=15
  • p=0.30
  • r=8
  • P(r=8)

Finding the probability

The following shows how to get the probability of 8 or more members not voting i.e P(8<=r<=15)

import numpy as np

f = 0

for x in range(8,15):

  f += sum(np.random.binomial(15, 0.30, 20000) == x)/20000

print(f)
0.0504

Mean, Variance and Standard Deviation

from scipy.stats import binom

m, v = binom.stats(10,0.2, loc=0, moments='mv')

The binom class takes on two parameters, n and p in the code above, in this case we are finding the mean and variance where n=10 and p=0.2.

from math import sqrt

 m,v,sqrt(v)

(array(2.0), array(1.6), 1.2649110640673518)

Another method for finding mean and variance of a binomial distribution

Mean of a binomial distribution

binom.mean(10,0.2)

2.0

Variance of a binomial distribution

binom.var(10,0.2)

1.6000000000000001

Standard Deviation of a binomial distribution

binom.std(10,0.2)

1.2649110640673518
